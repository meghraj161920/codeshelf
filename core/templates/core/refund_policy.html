Transparency Policy

Refund & Cancellation

Clear, fair guidelines regarding digital asset purchases, seller commissions, and service cancellations.

Digital Goods

Once source code is downloaded or accessed, it is generally non-refundable due to the nature of digital intellectual property.

48-Hour Window

Refund requests for corrupted files or mismatched descriptions must be submitted within 48 hours of purchase.

Seller Protection

We protect our creators by verifying claims before reversing any payments made to their seller wallets.

Our Commitment to Fairness

At CodeShelf, we act as a bridge between high-quality developers and eager learners. This policy ensures that customers receive what they paid for, while creators are compensated fairly for their hard work.

The Golden Rule

We review every dispute manually. If the code is broken or the description is misleading, we will make it right.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I get a refund if I change my mind?

Because our products are digital source codes that can be copied instantly, we do not offer refunds for "change of mind" once the files have been downloaded from your dashboard.

2. What if the code doesn't run on my machine?

Every project includes a README file. If you follow the steps and it still fails, please contact the Seller first. If they don't respond within 24 hours, CodeShelf support will intervene.

3. When are refunds approved?

Refunds are typically approved if:

  • The ZIP file is corrupted or empty.
  • The tech stack mentioned (e.g., React) is completely different from the actual code (e.g., HTML).
  • Critical features mentioned in the description are missing.

Step 2

Step 3

Receive a resolution or refund within 5-7 business days.
